Lime Cheese Salad

Description

A large, donut-shaped, bright green jelly dish that uses lime-flavoured, sweet Jello-O mixed with vinegar & cottage cheese as a base and is then stuffed with indistiguishable brown 'seafood salad' (Prawns? Deep fried battered fish? The choice is yours!). So get out your 1950s jelly moulds, put some Frank Sinatra on the record player, and smoke a pack of camels to prepare yourself and your taste buds for this one!

DISCLAIMER: The term 'salad' here seems to be used to refer to any mix of foods whatsoever and should not be confused with the conventional use of the word 'salad' to refer to a dish made up of predominantly raw fruits and vegetables. i.e. Please do not confuse your doctor's advice to 'eat more salad' with consuming this abomination.

Ingredients

  • 1 package lime Jello-O
  • 2T vinegar
  • 1t grated onion
  • 1 cup cottage cheese
  • 1T mayonnaise
  • salad greens (for garnish)
  • seafood salad (get creative)

Steps

  1. Dissolve 1 package lime Jello-O in 1 cup hot water.
  2. Add 3/4 cup cold water, vinegar, onion. Mix.
  3. Pour 1/2 cup mixture into 1-quart ring mould. Chill until firm.
  4. Chill remaining Jello-O mix until slightly thickened, then fold in cottage cheese & mayo. Blend.
  5. Pour blended mayo onto firm Jello-O.
  6. Push out of moulds. Garnish with salad greens. Fill centre with seafood salad.
